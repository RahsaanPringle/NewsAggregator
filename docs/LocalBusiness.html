Advertiser acquisition plan

Turn the directory into founding sponsor revenue

Start with businesses that already benefit from visitors, lake traffic, meals, gifts, and weekend plans.

Use Pipeline

1. Lead with local intent

Sell visibility to people actively looking for where to eat, shop, listen to music, buy gifts, and plan lake days.

2. Offer founding deals

Give the first 10 advertisers lower rates, bonus directory placement, and input on launch categories.

3. Remove creative friction

Include basic ad setup: logo, photo, short offer, button text, and link. Make buying easier than boosting a post.

4. Report simply

Send a monthly recap: days live, placements, views, clicks, and any featured content performance.

Best first targets

Restaurants and coffee shops

They can promote weekend specials, live music, brunch, lake-day meals, and visitor-friendly offers.

Shopping, gifts, and boutiques

They benefit from discovery, seasonal gift guides, visitor traffic, and "things to do near SML" content.

Lake life and wineries

They fit destination planning and can buy category sponsorships around weekends, events, and tourism seasons.

How this compares

Channel Common buying model Small-business reality Our angle
Google search ads Pay per click Strong intent, but competitive terms can get expensive and require active setup. Lower-friction local visibility plus directory content.
Facebook/Instagram boosts CPM or clicks Easy to start, but attention is mixed with every other social distraction. Reach readers already browsing local recommendations and lake plans.
Local newspaper or magazine Display ad size, run dates, column inches, or package rate Trusted format, but print runs are less editable and reporting can be limited. Digital placement with fast creative changes and simple click reporting.
Chamber/directory listing Annual membership or listing fee Good credibility, but not always built around daily content. Pair directory placement with news, events, deals, and visitor intent.

Benchmark notes: CPM is a standard way to compare ad exposure, newspaper display ads are traditionally sold by placement size, and column inches explain older newspaper pricing mechanics. Use these as comparison language, then validate exact local competitor rates before publishing a final rate card.

Minimum proof packet

  • Traffic snapshot: monthly page views, top pages, and returning visitors if available.
  • Local intent snapshot: searches, directory clicks, event/deal clicks, and category views.
  • Audience fit: Moneta/SML positioning, business categories, and visitor-focused use cases.
  • Sample advertiser report: placement, days live, impressions, clicks, CTR, and creative shown.
  • Founding advertiser offer: discounted first-month package with clear renewal pricing.
