Positioning

What We Are Building

NewsAggregator should become a fast, organized news site that blends aggregation, ranking, summaries, reader comments, and advertiser-friendly sections. The public site should feel like a practical news product, while the admin dashboard remains the control room for collection, moderation, analytics, and ad operations.

The business starts simple: prove repeatable traffic and local advertiser interest before building expensive newsroom infrastructure. The site earns trust by linking to original sources, labeling sponsored content, and using the database to keep the experience fast and measurable.

Sample advertiser promise
"Reach engaged readers at the moment they are browsing daily headlines, business updates, and developing stories. Buy a weekly sponsor package with transparent placement and simple performance reporting."

Roadmap

Phased Plan From Concept To Real Website

0 to 30 days

Foundation

Turn the dashboard prototype into a stable publishing system.

  • Keep all feed refreshes server-side and database-first.
  • Create public article/category pages from the MySQL article store.
  • Define editorial labels: aggregated, curated, sponsored, commentary, and original.
  • Add site navigation for World, Business, Coverage, Trending, and Opinion.
  • Document the minimum viable publishing workflow.
30 to 60 days

Audience

Build habits, sharing loops, and trust signals.

  • Add daily summary pages and source distribution transparency.
  • Improve article comments with moderation and abuse handling.
  • Add newsletter signup and weekly digest templates.
  • Track page views, outbound clicks, searches, and comment engagement.
  • Create a public "Advertise With Us" page with starter packages.
60 to 90 days

Revenue

Make ad inventory concrete, measurable, and sellable.

  • Create ad slots in header, sidebar, article body, newsletter, and sponsored story cards.
  • Build a simple ad campaign table with advertiser, placement, dates, creative URL, and status.
  • Add admin reporting for impressions, clicks, CTR, and active campaigns.
  • Prepare a one-page media kit for local advertisers.
  • Sell a pilot package to 3 to 5 early advertisers.
90 days and beyond

Scale

Turn the prototype into a repeatable media operation.

  • Add original reporting or contributor posts where the site can own unique value.
  • Segment ad inventory by category, geography, device, and newsletter audience.
  • Automate advertiser invoices, creative approvals, and campaign summaries.
  • Introduce SEO pages, structured data, sitemap generation, and archive pages.
  • Evaluate memberships only after repeat traffic and advertiser demand are visible.

Operating Rules

Ad Standards Before Selling

  • Every paid placement must be labeled as sponsored, advertisement, or partner content.
  • Ads should never impersonate editorial headlines or source links.
  • Campaign dates, placement names, advertiser contact, and creative files should be tracked in the database.
  • Advertiser reporting should use simple metrics first: impressions, clicks, CTR, and active days.
  • Reject ads that damage trust, confuse readers, or create legal/compliance risk.

Implementation Notes

How To Move The Codebase

Near-term repo plan

  1. Keep sb-admin-react as the internal dashboard and operational console.
  2. Use news-api for reusable data APIs or consolidate server code if duplication appears.
  3. Add a public frontend path or app once the data contracts are stable.
  4. Keep article reads database-first and refresh feeds in the background.
  5. Create a small ad-serving module before adding third-party ad networks.

Suggested first ad tables

advertisers(id, name, contact_email, website_url, status) ad_campaigns(id, advertiser_id, name, starts_at, ends_at, budget, status) ad_placements(id, code, label, page_area, dimensions, is_active) ad_creatives(id, campaign_id, placement_id, image_url, headline, target_url) ad_events(id, creative_id, event_type, page_url, occurred_at)
Decision Log

Open Business Decisions

Decision Recommendation Why It Matters
Public brand name Keep NewsAggregator as working name until visual identity is chosen. Prevents brand polish from blocking product proof.
Initial geography Start broad with national/world feeds, then add local focus where advertiser relationships exist. Local ad sales work best when the audience promise is specific.
Ad sales model Sell direct sponsorships before programmatic ads. Direct packages are easier to explain, control, and validate early.
Original content Add it after the public site and ad slots are working. Original reporting is valuable, but it increases operating cost.
Reader accounts Keep comments lightweight first, then add accounts only when moderation needs demand it. Reduces friction while the audience is still forming.
